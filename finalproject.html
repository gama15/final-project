    Michael Jordan

Michael Jordan Although Jordan abruptly retired from basketball before the beginning of the 1993–94 NBA season to pursue a new venture in minor league baseball, he returned to the Bulls in March 1995 and led them to three additional championships in 1996, 1997, and 1998, as well as a then-record 72 regular-season wins in the 1995–96. Jordan's individual accolades and accomplishments include five Most Valuable Player (MVP) Awards (1988, 1991, 1992, 1996, 1998), ten All-NBA First Team designations (1987-1993, 1996-1998), nine All-Defensive First Team honors (1988-1993, 1996-1998), fourteen NBA All-Star Game appearances, three All-Star Game MVP Awards, ten scoring titles, three steals titles, six NBA Finals MVP Awards, and the 1988 NBA Defensive Player of the Year Award. Among his numerous accomplishments, Jordan holds the NBA records for highest career regular season scoring average (30.12 points per game) and highest career playoff scoring average (33.45 points per game).
